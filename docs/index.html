Copy
History.

A private clipboard history for iPhone. Anything you copy while the app is open stays here, on-device, so you can find it again — quietly, with no account, no server, no telemetry.

v1.0.0·iPhone·iOS 16+

01

Overview

How it works

While Copy History is open, anything you copy from another app is captured automatically — no setup, no sign-in, no permissions to grant beyond iOS's standard paste prompt.

  • Tap any entry to copy it back to the clipboard.
  • Pin entries (★) you want to keep — pinned items aren't evicted when the 500-entry cap is reached.
  • Save reusable snippets (email, phone, address) above the history. Saved snippets stay forever until you delete them.
  • If an entry looks like a URL, email, or phone number, an inline action appears so you can open it directly.
02

iOS behavior

Why does iOS show a paste banner?

iOS shows a "Copy History pasted from…" banner whenever any app reads the clipboard. This is an iOS-wide privacy feature — Copy History can't disable it.

You can stop the banners by going to Settings → Apps → Copy History → Paste from Other Apps → Allow. The option appears after iOS prompts you the first time.

03

iOS behavior

Why aren't copies captured when the app is closed?

iOS does not allow apps to read the clipboard in the background. Copy History only captures while the app is in the foreground. Bring the app to the front when you want to save something new.

04

Data

Where is my history stored?

All entries live on your device only. Nothing is sent to a server, nothing is synced to iCloud, nothing is shared with third parties. See the privacy policy for the full account.
