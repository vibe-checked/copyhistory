Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 28, 2026

Copy History is designed to be private by default. All data stays on your device. Nothing is transmitted, stored remotely, or shared with third parties.

What data the app handles

  • Clipboard contents (text only) that you copy while the app is in the foreground.
  • Saved snippets that you create manually (label + text).
  • Pin state for individual entries.

How that data is stored

  • On your device only, in the app's local storage (iOS sandboxed storage via React Native AsyncStorage).
  • The most recent 500 clipboard entries are retained; pinned entries and saved snippets are never auto-evicted.
  • Clearing history or deleting individual entries from within the app removes them permanently from your device.
  • Uninstalling the app removes all stored data.

What is NOT collected or transmitted

  • No analytics or telemetry of any kind.
  • No advertising identifiers.
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data.
  • No account, no login, no user profile.
  • No network requests for clipboard data — nothing leaves your device.

Permissions

iOS requires explicit user permission to read the clipboard. You will see iOS's "pasted from…" banner and/or paste-permission prompts whenever the app reads the clipboard. You can change the Paste from Other Apps setting at any time in Settings → Apps → Copy History.

Children's privacy

Copy History is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children under 13. It does not knowingly collect any data that could identify a user of any age, because it does not collect or transmit personal data.

Changes to this policy

If material changes are made, the "Last updated" date above will change. Continued use of the app after an update constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
